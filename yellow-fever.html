ABOUT YELLOW FEVER

What is yellow fever?

Yellow fever is a serious viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes in tropical regions of Africa and South America. The vaccine provides lifelong protection and many countries require proof of vaccination for entry.

Why vaccination matters:

  • Required for entry to many countries
  • Lifelong protection with single dose
  • Internationally recognised certificate provided
  • Protects against serious illness

DO YOU NEED IT?

Who needs yellow fever vaccination?

You need yellow fever if:

  • Travelling to countries where yellow fever is present
  • Countries requiring certificate for entry
  • Travelling from yellow fever zone to another country

Countries requiring yellow fever:

  • Most of sub-Saharan Africa
  • Parts of South America
  • Some countries require it from all travellers

CONTRAINDICATIONS

Who should not have yellow fever vaccine?

Pregnant women

Not recommended during pregnancy

Breastfeeding mothers

Avoid if breastfeeding infants under 9 months

Babies under 6 months

Not suitable for infants under 6 months

Severe egg allergy

Vaccine contains egg protein

Weakened immune system

Including HIV, cancer treatment, immunosuppressants

Thymus gland disorders

Including myasthenia gravis, thymoma

Note: If you have any of these conditions, we'll discuss alternatives during your consultation
